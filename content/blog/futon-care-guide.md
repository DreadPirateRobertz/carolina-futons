A well-maintained futon lasts over a decade. A neglected one shows wear in a year. The difference is simple maintenance that takes minutes per month. Here's your complete care guide.

## Mattress Care

### Monthly: Vacuum

Use an upholstery attachment to vacuum the entire mattress surface. This removes dust, allergens, and small debris that work their way into the fabric and accelerate wear. Pay extra attention to seams and crevices where crumbs collect.

### Quarterly: Air Out

On a dry, sunny day, stand the mattress upright or prop it near a window for 4-6 hours. UV light kills bacteria and dust mites, while air circulation removes trapped moisture. This is especially important in humid climates — the Carolinas included.

### Every 3-6 Months: Rotate

Rotate your mattress head-to-foot to distribute wear evenly. Our Otis Bed mattresses feature no-turn design, so rotation is all you need — no flipping. If your mattress doesn't have a designated top and bottom, flip it as well.

### Spot Cleaning

Act immediately on spills. Blot (don't rub) with a clean cloth. Mix a small amount of mild dish detergent with cool water, apply with a cloth, then blot dry. Never soak the mattress — excess moisture promotes mold and degrades foam.

For stubborn stains: sprinkle baking soda, let it sit for 30 minutes, then vacuum. This works on most food, drink, and body oil stains.

### What NOT to Do

- Never machine wash or dry-clean a futon mattress
- Never use bleach, hydrogen peroxide, or harsh chemicals on the fabric
- Never fold the mattress wet — it will develop mold inside
- Never use a steamer — the moisture penetrates too deeply

## Frame Care

### Solid Wood Frames

**Monthly:** Dust with a soft, dry cloth. Microfiber works best.

**Quarterly:** Check all bolts, screws, and nuts. Daily use gradually loosens hardware. Tighten with the appropriate tool — most Night & Day frames use standard hex bolts.

**Annually:** Apply furniture polish or wood conditioner to maintain the finish. Natural beeswax polish is excellent for hardwood frames. Avoid silicone-based sprays — they leave residue.

**Placement:** Keep frames at least 6 inches from heating vents and out of direct sunlight. Heat dries wood, causing cracks. Sun fades finishes unevenly.

### Metal Frames

**Monthly:** Wipe with a damp cloth and dry immediately to prevent rust.

**Quarterly:** Check all bolts and joints. Lubricate squeaky pivot points with a drop of 3-in-1 oil or white lithium grease.

**As needed:** Touch up paint chips with matching spray paint to prevent rust from forming.

## Cover Care

Removable futon covers are your mattress's first line of defense. Keep them clean and they'll protect your investment for years.

### Washing

Most removable covers are machine washable:
1. Remove from mattress
2. Close all zippers
3. Wash on gentle cycle, cold water
4. Use mild detergent — no bleach
5. Tumble dry on low or air dry

**Frequency:** Wash every 1-2 months, or immediately after spills.

### Cover Types

**Cotton/twill covers:** Most durable, machine washable, breathable. They soften with washing.

**Microfiber/microsuede:** Stain-resistant, soft feel, machine washable. Best for homes with kids or pets.

**Denim:** Extremely durable, casual look, machine washable. Gets better with age.

### When to Replace

Replace covers when they develop permanent stains, thin spots, or broken zippers. A worn cover exposes your mattress to direct soiling and wear. Replacement covers cost $40-$100 — far cheaper than a new mattress.

## Seasonal Maintenance Checklist

### Spring
- Deep vacuum mattress and frame
- Air mattress in sunshine
- Wash cover
- Tighten all frame hardware
- Check for winter moisture damage

### Summer
- Use a lightweight cover (cotton/twill breathes best)
- Rotate mattress
- Keep frame away from AC vents (condensation)

### Fall
- Switch to a heavier cover if desired
- Deep clean before hosting season
- Check frame finish for summer sun damage

### Winter
- Keep frame away from heating sources
- Use a mattress protector under sheets (winter bedding traps more moisture)
- Rotate mattress

## FAQ Section

**How do you clean a futon mattress?**
Vacuum monthly. Spot-clean stains with mild detergent and a damp cloth. Air in sunlight quarterly. Never soak the mattress.

**How often should you flip a futon mattress?**
Rotate head-to-foot every 3-6 months. Our Otis Bed mattresses have no-turn design — rotation only.

**How do you keep a futon from sagging?**
Use a solid platform or closely spaced slats, rotate the mattress regularly, vary sitting positions, and choose high-density mattresses (8+ inches).

**Can you wash a futon cover in the washing machine?**
Most removable covers are machine washable — gentle cycle, cold water, tumble dry low.

**How do you maintain a wood futon frame?**
Dust regularly, tighten hardware every 6 months, apply furniture polish annually, keep away from direct sunlight and heat.
