Box springs were invented to extend mattress life and add height to beds. Modern mattresses don't need either. Platform beds provide complete support with built-in slats, save you money on a box spring, and deliver a cleaner, lower-profile look that fits today's bedrooms.

## What Is a Platform Bed?

A platform bed is a bed frame with a built-in support system — either solid slats, closely spaced slats, or a solid platform surface. The mattress sits directly on this surface. No box spring. No foundation. Just the frame and your mattress.

The result: a lower, cleaner bed profile that provides firm, even support and works with every modern mattress type.

## Why Box Springs Are Obsolete

Box springs were designed in the early 1900s for innerspring mattresses that needed extra give. They added bounce and raised the bed to a comfortable height. Two problems:

**Modern mattresses don't need bounce.** Memory foam, latex, and hybrid mattresses are engineered to provide their own support and responsiveness. A box spring adds unnecessary flex that can actually reduce mattress support.

**Box springs can damage foam mattresses.** The wire grid inside a box spring creates uneven pressure points. Over time, this can cause foam mattresses to develop impressions and wear unevenly. Platform slats distribute weight more evenly.

**The cost is unnecessary.** A quality box spring costs $100-$300. A platform bed eliminates this expense entirely and provides better support.

## Platform Bed Benefits

### Better Mattress Support

Closely spaced slats (2-3 inches apart) provide consistent, even support across the entire mattress surface. This is especially important for foam and hybrid mattresses, which need a solid, flat surface to perform as designed.

### Lower Profile

Platform beds sit lower than traditional bed + box spring combinations. This creates a modern, streamlined look and makes small bedrooms feel less cluttered. Low-profile beds are also easier to get in and out of for children and older adults.

### Cost Savings

Eliminating the box spring saves $100-$300 immediately. Over the life of the bed, you also save on replacement box springs (which wear out every 8-10 years).

### Improved Airflow

Slatted platforms allow air to circulate beneath the mattress, reducing moisture buildup that can lead to mold, mildew, and premature mattress degradation. This is especially important in humid climates.

### Storage Space

Many platform beds include built-in drawers or have enough clearance underneath for storage bins. This can eliminate the need for a separate dresser in smaller bedrooms.

## What Mattress to Use

**Memory foam:** Excellent on platforms. The solid surface lets the foam do its job without interference. Choose 8+ inches for best results.

**Latex:** Natural or hybrid latex works perfectly on slats. The even support enhances latex's inherent responsiveness.

**Hybrid (coil + foam):** Great on platforms. The coil base provides its own internal support, and the platform keeps everything stable.

**Innerspring:** Works on platforms with closely spaced slats (3 inches max). Wide slat spacing can cause coil mattresses to feel uneven.

**Futon mattresses:** Not ideal. Futon mattresses are designed to fold and don't perform their best on fixed platform beds. Use a traditional mattress instead.

## Shopping Checklist

Before buying a platform bed, verify:

- **Slat spacing:** 3 inches maximum for universal mattress compatibility
- **Center support:** Beds Queen-size and larger should have at least one center support leg or beam
- **Weight capacity:** Check the manufacturer's rating against your needs
- **Headboard attachment:** Built-in or separate? Does it attach to the wall or the frame?
- **Assembly difficulty:** How many pieces? What tools are needed? Read reviews for assembly feedback
- **Under-bed clearance:** If you want storage underneath, measure the clearance height
- **Mattress compatibility:** Confirm your mattress type is supported (avoid very thin mattresses under 6 inches)

## FAQ Section

**Do you need a box spring with a platform bed?**
No. Platform beds have built-in slats that provide complete mattress support. Adding a box spring is unnecessary and raises the bed too high.

**What type of mattress works best on a platform bed?**
Memory foam, latex, hybrid, and innerspring all work. The flat, even slat support is especially good for foam mattresses.

**Are platform beds good for back support?**
Yes. Consistent, even support prevents sagging and maintains spinal alignment. Some models offer adjustable slat tension for customizable firmness.

**How much weight can a platform bed hold?**
Quality solid wood: 500-1,000 lbs. Metal: 300-500 lbs. Always check manufacturer specifications.

**Do platform beds squeak?**
Solid wood platforms rarely squeak. Metal platforms are more prone to noise at connection points. Tighten bolts and add felt pads between metal contacts if squeaking occurs.
