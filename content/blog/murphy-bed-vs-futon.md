Two furniture categories dominate the space-saving conversation: futons and Murphy beds. Both convert between day and night use. Both reclaim floor space. But they solve the problem in very different ways. Here's how to choose.

## How They Work

### Futons

A futon frame holds a flexible mattress that folds from a flat bed into an upright sofa position. During the day, you have a couch. At night, you unfold it into a full or queen-size bed. The conversion takes about 15 seconds.

### Murphy Cabinet Beds

A Murphy cabinet bed stores a folding mattress inside an elegant cabinet. When closed, it looks like a piece of furniture — a chest, credenza, or nightstand. When you need a bed, you pull the mattress out of the cabinet and it unfolds flat onto the floor or a built-in platform. Setup takes about 90 seconds.

**Important distinction:** Our Murphy cabinet beds are NOT wall-mounted Murphy beds. They're freestanding cabinets that require zero installation. No wall bolts, no construction, no landlord approval needed.

## When to Choose a Futon

**You need daytime seating.** Futons are sofas first, beds second. If you need a couch in your living room that converts to a guest bed, a futon is the obvious choice.

**Budget is a factor.** A quality futon frame and mattress starts around $500. You can get an excellent setup for under $1,000.

**You convert frequently.** Futons fold and unfold in seconds. If you switch between sofa and bed daily, the speed and simplicity matter.

**You have a studio apartment.** A futon gives you both a couch and a bed in one piece. It's the most space-efficient way to furnish a studio that needs both.

## When to Choose a Murphy Cabinet Bed

**You want the bed to disappear.** When closed, a Murphy cabinet bed is a beautiful piece of furniture. Guests won't know there's a bed in the room. This is ideal for home offices and multi-use rooms.

**Sleep quality is the top priority.** Murphy cabinet beds include premium Queen gel memory foam mattresses. The sleeping experience is closer to a traditional bed than any convertible.

**You have a dedicated guest room.** A guest room with a Murphy cabinet bed functions as an office, craft room, or exercise space when guests aren't visiting. The bed takes up zero floor space when stored.

**You don't need a sofa.** If the room already has seating (or doesn't need it), a Murphy cabinet bed uses floor space more efficiently.

## Space Analysis

**Futon in sofa mode:** Sits about 36" deep, 80" wide. Takes up roughly the same space as a standard sofa. You can walk around it and use the room normally.

**Murphy cabinet closed:** About 24" deep, 64" wide. Takes up less than half the floor space of a futon-as-sofa. The room feels more open.

**Futon as bed:** A Queen futon takes 60" x 80" of floor space — the bed IS the sofa, just flat.

**Murphy cabinet as bed:** The mattress extends from the cabinet, taking 60" x 80" of floor space PLUS the 24" depth of the cabinet behind it. Total footprint is larger than a futon-as-bed.

**Bottom line:** Murphy beds save more space during the day. Futons save more space at night.

## FAQ Section

**Is a Murphy bed or futon better for a small apartment?**
Depends on priorities. Futons are more affordable and provide seating. Murphy cabinet beds hide completely when closed, maximizing open floor space.

**Do Murphy cabinet beds need to be mounted to the wall?**
No. Our Murphy cabinet beds are completely freestanding. No wall mounting, no installation, no tools.

**Which is more comfortable for sleeping?**
Murphy cabinet beds with their included Queen gel memory foam mattress provide a more traditional sleeping experience. High-quality futons with 8-10 inch mattresses come very close.

**How much space does a Murphy cabinet bed save?**
A closed Murphy cabinet bed takes about 10 square feet (24" x 64"). When closed, it functions as furniture. Compare to a futon-as-sofa at 20-24 square feet.
